Utah Ski Resorts — Quick Guide

Short terrain descriptions, locations, and on-site amenities for popular Utah ski areas.

❄️

Alta

Location: Little Cottonwood Canyon — ~35 minutes from Salt Lake City

Alta is a skier-only mountain famous for its deep, light powder and challenging terrain. Expect steep chutes, wide-open bowls, and a lift system that accesses classic backcountry-esque lines. The resort keeps a strong traditional vibe — no snowboard access.

Ski-only
Powder & bowls

Snowbird

Location: Little Cottonwood Canyon — adjacent to Alta

Snowbird offers steep, varied terrain and a legendary vertical drop with long, sustained runs. It’s a playground for experts and adventurous intermediates, with terrain parks, gladed runs, and access to sidecountry — plus modern base-area facilities and a tram that climbs to high alpine terrain.

Aerial tram
Advanced & expert terrain
Spa & lodging
Terrain parks

Park City

Location: Park City — ~35–45 minutes from Salt Lake City

Park City Mountain is one of the largest ski areas in the U.S., offering terrain for every ability level: long groomers, cruisers, bowls, and several terrain parks. It has an extensive lift network, lively base village with shops and restaurants, and direct access to town for nightlife and lodging.

Extensive lift network
Family-friendly

Après & shops

Deer Valley

Location: Park City — premium, ski-only resort

Deer Valley is known for its groomed runs, impeccable service, and skier-first experience. Expect refined on-mountain dining, high-quality snow grooming, and top-notch guest services. It’s a great choice for families and guests looking for a higher-end resort experience.

Ski-only
Groomed cruisers
Fine dining
Ski valets

Brighton

Location: Big Cottonwood Canyon — family and snowboard-friendly

Brighton is a laid-back, fun-focused mountain popular with families, locals, and snowboarders. It has a friendly vibe, great night-skiing options, varied terrain, and affordable lift options. The terrain parks and easy access from the city make it a local favorite.
